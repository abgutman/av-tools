What am I looking at? — SEC form definitions

Press release

The earnings announcement on the wire (BusinessWire / GlobeNewswire / PR Newswire) and the company's IR page. First to drop. Usually before market open or after market close. What you cite for the lead.

8-K item 2.02

The SEC wrapper around the earnings press release. Same content as the wire release. Drops on EDGAR a few minutes to a few hours after the wire. The press release itself is attached as exhibit 99.1.

10-Q / 10-K

Full quarterly (10-Q) or annual (10-K) report — the deep dive with footnotes, risk factors, segment breakouts, litigation. Often filed same day as the 8-K but sometimes 1-15 days later. The story behind the headline.

Watch window

For each company, we look at how many days after each fiscal-quarter end they actually filed their last 4 quarterly reports. The min-max range is when the scraper watches the IR page, wire feed, and EDGAR for the next release.

Filing pattern (A / B/C / D)

A = pre-market filer (6:00-7:30 AM). Call same morning at 8:30-10 AM. Easy to staff.
B/C = post-market filer (4:00-4:45 PM). Call either same evening (10 min later, unstaffable) or next morning (16 hrs later, staffable).
D = mid-day filer (10 AM-noon). Mostly REITs and utilities.

Save-the-date

When companies pre-announce the date and time of their next earnings call. Scraped from the wire and from specific IR events pages on Mon/Wed/Fri evenings. The most reliable way to staff a Pattern C call. Empty column for now — coming in Phase 2c.
