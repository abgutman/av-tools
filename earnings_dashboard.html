What am I looking at? — SEC form definitions

Press release

The earnings announcement that hits the wire (BusinessWire / GlobeNewswire / PR Newswire) and the company's IR page. First to drop — usually before market open or after market close. Contains headline revenue/profit figures and a CEO quote. What you cite for the lead.

8-K item 2.02

The SEC wrapper around the earnings press release. Same content as the wire release, just stamped "official." Drops on EDGAR a few minutes to a few hours after the wire. The press release itself is attached as exhibit 99.1; the slide deck (if any) as exhibit 99.2.

10-Q

Full quarterly report (covers Q1, Q2, or Q3). The deep dive — segment breakouts, footnotes, risk-factor updates, litigation disclosures, off-balance-sheet items. Often filed same day as the 8-K but sometimes a few days later. What you read for the story behind the headline.

10-K

Annual report (replaces the Q4 10-Q). Audited financials, full risk factors, exec comp tables, properties list, related-party transactions. Mid-Feb to early March for most calendar-year filers. The most reporter-rich filing each year.

Other 8-Ks

Material events that aren't earnings — M&A announcements (item 1.01), executive departures (item 5.02), debt issuance (item 2.03), lawsuits (item 8.01), Reg FD disclosures (item 7.01). Tracked separately on the Other 8-Ks page.

Watch window

For each company, we look at how many days after each fiscal-quarter end they actually filed their last 4 quarterly reports. The min of those gaps is the earliest the next report can plausibly drop; the max is the latest. The window is when the scraper should start watching the IR page, wire feed, and EDGAR.
